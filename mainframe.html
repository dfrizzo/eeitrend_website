Overview

The Mainframe Is Not Going Away

The mainframe remains the backbone of the world's most critical enterprise systems—banking, insurance, government, healthcare, and logistics. Decades of mission-critical logic, proven reliability, and unmatched throughput make it irreplaceable in ways that cloud-only architects often underestimate.

But the mainframe of today is not the mainframe of twenty years ago. IBM z/OS continues to evolve rapidly—with native AI acceleration, hybrid cloud connectivity, and modern development tooling that bridges the gap between legacy COBOL workloads and the broader enterprise technology landscape.

The real challenge isn't whether to keep the mainframe—it's how to modernize around it intelligently. That means extending its value with AI, integrating it into hybrid cloud architectures, developing the next generation of mainframe practitioners, and building application strategies that respect both the platform's strengths and the organization's future direction.

Focus Areas

Where I Work

IBM z/OS & Enterprise Architecture

Strategic architecture for z/OS environments—workload placement, platform governance, capacity strategy, and aligning the mainframe's role within the broader enterprise architecture. Ensuring the platform is positioned for long-term value, not just near-term operation.

Optimizing AI with the IBM Mainframe

Bringing AI capabilities to where the data actually lives. Leveraging IBM's AI accelerators and z/OS integration points to run inference, automate operations, and modernize workloads without moving critical data off-platform. Practical AI for mainframe environments—not theoretical.

Mainframe Application Development Practice & Strategy

Building and maturing mainframe development practices for the modern era—modern toolchains, CI/CD integration, developer experience improvements, workforce development, and knowledge transfer strategies that address the talent gap without abandoning institutional expertise.

COBOL & Legacy Application Strategy

Honest, pragmatic guidance on COBOL application portfolios—what to modernize, what to refactor, what to leave alone, and what to retire. Avoiding the pitfall of expensive, high-risk rewrites in favor of incremental strategies that deliver value without destabilizing proven systems.

Hybrid Cloud & Legacy Coexistence

Designing integration patterns that let mainframe systems participate fully in a hybrid cloud architecture—API enablement, event streaming, data virtualization, and identity federation. The mainframe as a first-class citizen in a multi-cloud world, not an island apart from it.
